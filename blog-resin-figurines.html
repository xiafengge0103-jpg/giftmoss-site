Custom Resin Figurines: Build Your Brand with OEM Manufacturing

Published July 12, 2026 · 7 min read

Custom resin figurines are one of the highest-margin product categories in the gift and souvenir industry. From zoo gift shops stocking branded animal figurines to boutique retailers selling seasonal decorations under their own label, OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturing) resin products create lasting brand value and customer loyalty that generic products cannot match.

Why Custom Resin Figurines Are a Smart B2B Investment

  • High perceived value: Hand-painted resin products feel premium. Customers willingly pay $15–$50 for a figurine that costs $3–$8 to manufacture.
  • Brand lock-in: A custom-designed figurine cannot be found on Amazon or AliExpress. When your design is unique, customers must come back to you.
  • Year-round demand: Souvenir shops, tourist attractions, museums, and gift stores purchase figurines continuously — not just during holiday seasons.
  • Seasonal refreshes: New designs for Christmas, Halloween, Easter, and spring collections create recurring reorder cycles from the same customers.

The OEM Process: From Concept to Container

Step 1: Design Submission

You provide the concept — a sketch, photograph, 3D model, or even a description. The factory's design team creates technical drawings for your approval. Most suppliers accept the following formats: .ai, .psd, .stl, .obj, or hand-drawn sketches with reference dimensions. If you do not have a design, many factories offer in-house design services for an additional fee.

Step 2: Mold Making

This is the biggest upfront investment in custom resin production. A silicone mold typically costs $200–$800 depending on complexity and size. The mold is reusable for thousands of units, so the per-unit mold cost becomes negligible at scale.

Key decision: Silicone molds (lower cost, ~500–1000 castings) vs. metal molds (higher cost, 10,000+ castings, finer detail). For most gift retailers, silicone molds are the right starting point.

Step 3: Sample Production

Before committing to bulk production, the factory produces 3–5 pre-production samples. You review these for color accuracy, painting quality, weight, and packaging. This stage typically takes 7–14 days. Never skip this step. Color matching on resin is subjective, and what looks right in a photo may differ from your brand palette.

Step 4: Bulk Production

Once samples are approved, bulk production begins. Typical timelines:

Order SizeProduction TimeNotes
500–1000 pcs15–20 daysStandard for boutique retailers
1000–5000 pcs20–30 daysMid-size gift shop chains
5000+ pcs30–45 daysLarge retail and tourist destinations

Step 5: Quality Control & Shipping

Final QC inspection covers: color consistency, paint adhesion, structural integrity, packaging condition, and quantity accuracy. For international buyers, third-party inspection services (SGS, Bureau Veritas) cost $200–$400 per inspection and are strongly recommended for first-time orders.

Popular Custom Figurine Categories

  • Animal figurines: Zoo and aquarium gift shops, pet stores, wildlife conservation organizations. Cats, dogs, elephants, and sea life are perennial bestsellers.
  • Holiday collections: Christmas Santa, snowman, reindeer sets; Halloween ghosts and pumpkins; Easter bunnies and chicks. Holiday figurines drive 40% of annual revenue for many gift retailers.
  • Brand mascots: Companies turn their logo characters into 3D collectible figurines for corporate gifting, trade show giveaways, and employee recognition.
  • Fantasy & pop culture: Dragons, fairies, wizards, and mythical creatures — strong sellers in comic shops, gaming stores, and online collectible marketplaces.
  • Memorial & pet figurines: Custom pet memorial figurines and personalized portrait statues are a growing niche with very high margins ($50–$150+ retail).

Pro Tip: Start with Stock Molds

If custom mold cost is a barrier, many factories offer stock molds you can use with custom painting. This gives you unique products at near-off-the-shelf pricing — ideal for first-time OEM buyers testing the waters.
